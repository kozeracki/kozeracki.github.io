## Environmental Permitting and Compliance Engineer
### Emilia Kozeracki  
### Intro to AI (DA 109)  

---

# Career Overview

- **Career goal:** Environmental Permitting and Compliance Engineer at a consulting firm  
- **Main purpose:** Ensure organizations follow environmental laws and permit requirements  
- **Typical tasks:**  
  - Prepare and submit environmental permit applications  
  - Monitor compliance data (air, water, and other environmental metrics)  
  - Evaluate environmental impact analyses  
  - Conduct site inspections and write technical reports  
- **Core skills:** Regulatory knowledge, environmental engineering calculations, data analysis, communication, and critical thinking

---

# AI Disruption in This Career

- AI can reduce repetitive administrative workload in permitting/compliance work
- Permit application workflows can be standardized and partially automated
- AI can reduce human error by checking forms for missing sections and inconsistencies
- Real-time AI monitoring tools can flag possible compliance violations faster
- The role may shift from manual paperwork toward oversight, strategy, and decision-making

---

# AI Benefits for Better Work

- AI can help compliance engineers spend more time on high-value judgment tasks
- Regulatory research can become faster through AI-supported summarization
- AI models can support environmental predictions and scenario analysis
- Monitoring alerts can improve response time and risk management
- AI can improve report clarity by helping translate technical findings for non-technical audiences

---

# Using AI to Gain an Advantage

- **Pre-submission QA:** Use AI to check permit packages before submission
- **Data interpretation:** Use tools (Excel/Python + AI support) to identify trends and explain results
- **Communication support:** Use AI drafting support to improve report clarity and professionalism
- **Stakeholder translation:** Use AI to create plain-language summaries for clients, regulators, and teams
- **Key mindset:** Use AI as a tool to improve performance, not as a replacement for professional judgment

---

# Final Takeaways and Next Steps

- AI is likely to **reshape** this career, not replace it
- Strong technical skills + strong communication skills remain essential
- Best preparation over the next year:
  1. Continue strengthening data/technical tools (Excel, Python, GIS, R)
  2. Improve technical writing and presentation skills
  3. Stay current on environmental regulations (ex: Clean Air Act, Clean Water Act)
- Long-term advantage: combine regulatory expertise with practical AI workflow skills
