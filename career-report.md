# Career Report

## 1. Career Overview

After I graduate with my degree in Environmental Engineering and minor in Data
Analytics, I will be working as an Environmental Permitting and Compliance
Engineer, specifically for a consulting firm. The overarching job is to ensure
organizations follow environmental laws and permits in order to operate legally.
Day-to-day tasks include preparing and submitting environmental permitting
applications for a variety of multimedia projects, ensuring compliance with
environmental laws (such as monitoring pollution level), evaluating
environmental impact analysis, and conducting on-site inspections.

This job requires a combination of technical skills and strong interpersonal
abilities. The most important would be to have a strong foundation in
environmental regulation and compliance knowledge; it is important to understand
the language and how to interpret and apply it. Having a background in technical
engineering skills for air emission or wastewater management calculations,
understanding environmental science principles, and being able to interpret
collected data is also essential.

This leads into data analysis. A large portion of this job is collecting
evidence in order to prove compliance, and this requires analyzing environmental
monitoring data, writing technical reports, and using common analysis tools such
as Excel or Python. Soft skills that are equally as important include having
strong communication skills, since this position acts as a mediator or
translator for other engineers, lawyers, construction workers, and people in
government. I would also consider time management, attention to detail, and
critical thinking to be important qualities for someone in this position.

## 2. AI Disruption

The majority of AI disruption for this career path would come from reducing
repetitive and administrative-heavy workload. AI tools can be utilized to
standardize and automate permit applications to reduce paperwork and human
errors that can be made. Real-time monitoring can also be implemented by the
use of AI that can flag violations.

## 3. AI Benefits

The AI disruptions can be interpreted as an advantage for individuals in this
career. As stated above, the repetitive work can be accurately managed by the
use of AI tools, which allows compliance engineers to focus on tasks that rely
on strategy, judgement, and problem solving, all of which require human insight.

Specific ways that AI would be advantageous include making regulatory research
easier by reducing manual reading, helping generate more accurate environmental
data predictions by creating AI simulation models, and implementing real-time
monitoring alerts. AI has the potential to reshape the role of an Environmental
Compliance and Regulation Engineer, but not to eliminate the position entirely.
I anticipate the position shifting from a more administrative role to a more
strategic-focused role.

## 4. Gaining an Advantage

Someone preparing for this position would stand out by integrating regulatory
knowledge with AI to work efficiently, improve communication, and reduce risk
and error. The most important aspect of using AI is to remember it is a tool and
not something to become fully reliant on.

The following examples reflect how using AI can create these improved traits.
AI can be used as a pre-check when submitting a permit application. The
application can be flagged for missing sections and inconsistencies that would
delay submission, and this can reduce the amount of revisions that need to be
made. From a technical standpoint, someone can also use AI to monitor data. Raw
environmental data can be uploaded to an AI tool, such as Python-based tooling,
and trend analysis or simple explanations and conclusions can be drawn. This
goes the extra step of not only reporting data, but interpreting it and
communicating it in a way that can be understood from a non-technical
perspective.

The best ways to prepare for this job would be to focus on and learn the
technological aspects that this profession can involve. As engineers, we are
well-versed in running calculations, understanding scientific principles,
knowing how to code, and running physical laboratory and site tests. The best
way to prepare for this shift from college to a job as a compliance engineer is
to understand how to implement all of these skills and, most importantly, how to
communicate effectively with people who do not have that technical background.
As stated above, AI tools can help bridge this initial gap, so it would be
essential to lean into that especially at the beginning of your career.

## 5. Action Plan

I will be providing three specific ways that over the next year I can better
prepare myself for an AI-influenced version of this career.

One step that I have already been implementing throughout the entirety of my
college career is building a strong foundation of technical data skills. Having
a minor in Data Analytics has really forced me to dive deep into this specialty,
and I already see the benefits and how well it incorporates into my major and my
future career. I have learned how to use Excel very well in terms of generating
and manipulating tables, data analysis, and charts. I have taken Python, GIS,
and R classes, and I have had the chance to explore and experiment from an
environmental standpoint. This exposure has made me more confident in my ability
to analyze and interpret data, not only generate a product.

The second step I want to take on would be working on communication. It is a bit
of a stereotype that engineers are not good writers simply due to lack of
experience and practice. I personally have taken one liberal arts class during
my four years of college, with the exception of the required classes for every
student at CUA. My classes are very exam-focused, so I admit I am out of
practice in terms of writing reports and papers. Using AI, especially during the
transition period from school to work, would be extremely beneficial for
improving my writing. It can be used to help with clarity, accuracy, and
creating a level of professionalism I am not yet accustomed to. AI can also be
used as a practice tool for simplifying complex findings.

The last step I would take to prepare for my upcoming career is staying updated
on environmental regulations. I am going to be moving into an environment where
everyone is an expert in this field and has a deep understanding and foundation
of common regulations. Studying and learning about key laws such as the Clean
Air Act and Clean Water Act can help me be more prepared. The nature of this
career is that it is constantly changing and fluctuating, so it would be a good
habit to start now to keep up with environmental news. AI can be helpful in
creating summaries and highlighting significant changes or updates that should be
made aware of.

Overall, over the course of the next year I should focus on practicing how to
implement AI into technical work, practicing my communication skills, and
creating a habit of staying updated and educated on news pertaining to my job.
